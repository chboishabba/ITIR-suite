# Legal Surface Expansion And Graph Union

Date: 2026-04-02

## Purpose

Pin the next moonshot-adjacent legal expansion round without pretending the
repo already "understands all law". The near-term job is to normalize AU legal
follow and related legal graph surfaces so they behave like compiler outputs:

- bounded evidence bundle in
- promoted outcomes out
- derived legal follow, packet, report, and graph surfaces after that

## Main Read

The long-range moonshot is broad:

- understand law across jurisdictions and languages
- compare systems without collapsing them into one invented truth
- surface commonalities, disjoint regions, and non-gluing basins
- let users inspect why a legal product was emitted

The immediate legal move is much smaller:

- expand AU case-follow and authority-follow surfaces
- make supporting legislation, instruments, citations, and authority receipts
  explicit derived products
- keep those products challengeable and replayable
- treat graphs as outputs over promoted or review-shaped material, not as
  silent truth engines

## AU Priority

AU is the best first legal adopter because the repo already has:

- bounded AustLII and JADE authority seams
- deterministic citation-follow
- AU semantic linkage and authority-receipt context
- AU fact-review bundle and operator workflow surfaces

So the next legal product should not be another side lane. It should be a
normalized AU legal-follow surface that turns the existing authority hints,
legal refs, and receipts into one explicit derived graph/product.

That product should answer:

- which event or fact is asking for authority follow?
- which case, statute, or instrument is being pointed at?
- what citation candidate exists?
- which authority receipt resolved part of that request?
- what still needs follow, and by which route?

## Wider Legal Moonshot Read

The same normalization rule should later apply outside AU:

- bounded legal evidence bundle in
- promoted facts, abstentions, and review pressure out
- derived legal follow/report/graph surfaces after that

This is the path toward a broader graph-union program over promoted material:

- keep jurisdiction-local promoted truth separate
- emit typed cross-system mapping and comparison products later
- make commonality, disjointness, and obstruction visible without claiming
  that the repo has solved "all law" already

## Governance Read

- ITIL:
  - define the legal service contract in terms of evidence in and products out
- ISO 9000:
  - output quality means replayable, inspectable, and bounded
- ISO 42001 / NIST AI RMF:
  - separate evidence, promoted outcomes, and derived graph products
- ISO 27001 / ISO 27701:
  - do not turn legal corpora into hidden cross-context surveillance
- ISO 23894:
  - core risks are hidden promotion, identity overreach, predictive drift,
    and graph overclaim
- Six Sigma:
  - reduce variance by normalizing legal-follow outputs instead of burying
    them in lane-local queue items
- C4/PlantUML:
  - keep one metasystem view that exposes compiler core, derived legal graph
    surfaces, and governance boundaries separately

## Immediate Implementation Target

Land one bounded AU derived product:

- `legal_follow_graph`

Its basis should be:

- authority receipt items
- follow-needed conjectures
- legal refs
- candidate neutral citations
- event-local authority hints

It should remain:

- derived
- non-causal by default
- non-predictive
- non-authoritative
- fit for operator inspection and follow-up routing
