# Anti-Panopticon Red-Team Provisions

Date: 2026-04-02

## Purpose

Make the anti-panopticon boundary operational for the expanding legal moonshot.
The repo already has the refusal doctrine. This note pins what red-team work
must actively test as legal and cross-system surfaces get stronger.

Primary governing docs:

- `SensibLaw/docs/panopticon_refusal.md`
- `SensibLaw/docs/judicial_decision_behavior_contract.md`
- `SensibLaw/docs/official_decision_behavior_contract.md`

## Non-Negotiable Provisions

The system must not become:

- a hidden surveillance archive
- a predictive judge, official, or citizen scoring engine
- a cross-context identity unifier by default
- a silent authority-upgrader that turns weak evidence into legal truth
- a graph that implies institutional or causal certainty just because a path
  can be drawn

## Required Red-Team Questions

Every stronger legal or graph surface should be tested against these questions:

1. Does it predict or score a person, judge, official, group, or institution?
2. Does it silently merge identities or roles across contexts?
3. Does it let graph reach or citation density masquerade as truth?
4. Does it infer legal meaning without an explicit evidence or receipt basis?
5. Does it hide abstention, uncertainty, or conflict?
6. Does it create a one-way legibility pipeline where subjects become readable
   to power without reciprocal visibility?

## Legal-Surface Specific Red Flags

For legal follow, case following, supporting legislation, and graph union work,
explicitly reject:

- predictive appeal-success or merits scoring
- behavioral forecasts about judges, advocates, litigants, officials, or
  institutions
- automated "risk" summaries about people
- cross-jurisdiction identity merging without explicit local anchors
- graph edges that imply a legal obligation, causal dependency, or doctrinal
  control relation without bounded textual or receipt support

Acceptable outputs are:

- explicit evidence bundles
- promoted outcomes with abstain and audit pressure visible
- derived legal follow graphs and reports with source anchoring
- route targets for manual or bounded deterministic follow

This applies equally to named proving grounds such as Brexit-era UK/EU legal
interaction work: the system may surface bounded legal-linkage and
cross-jurisdiction follow pressure there, but it must not turn controversial
political subjects into a surveillance or personality-ranking product.

## Required Boundary Labels

Any derived legal graph or union surface should say, in substance:

- derived from bounded evidence and receipts
- not itself the truth anchor
- not predictive
- not a person-scoring surface
- not a hidden identity-resolution surface

## Current Gap

The repo already has strong anti-panopticon doctrine, but the legal moonshot
notes and metasystem entrypoints did not yet say this explicitly enough.
