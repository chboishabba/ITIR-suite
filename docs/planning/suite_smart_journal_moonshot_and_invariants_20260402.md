# Suite Smart-Journal Moonshot and Invariants

Date: 2026-04-02

## Purpose

Re-state the moonshot at the whole-suite level rather than only through the
current `SensibLaw` legal/compiler lens.

The original trajectory was closer to a smart journal than to a single legal
engine:

- capture difficult reality
- preserve it without losing provenance
- compile usable state from it
- review and promote only what earns authority
- compare, reconcile, and union bounded products across domains
- keep the whole system auditable and anti-panopticon-safe

That remains the suite-level read.

## Suite-Level Moonshot

At moonshot, the suite acts like a compiler stack for difficult reality:

1. capture or ingest bounded source material
2. canonicalize identities, anchors, timestamps, envelopes, and lineage
3. compile read-only state and reviewable observations
4. follow justified dependencies and references in bounded ways
5. promote, abstain, or hold under explicit authority rules
6. derive comparison, graph, packet, report, and operator surfaces
7. union those derived products across domains, jurisdictions, languages, and
   time where justified
8. preserve challengeability, replay, and anti-panopticon constraints

This is broader than legal follow and broader than a single submodule.

The suite moonshot only works if those roles stay distinct.

## Constraints

Hard constraints across the suite:

- no hidden rewrite of source reality
- no authority crossing without explicit promotion receipts
- no silent conversion of derived overlays into canonical truth
- no context-free rendering of consequential outputs
- no second canonical reducer emerging accidentally in side systems
- no panopticon drift: more context is not permission for surveillance,
  prediction, coercive ranking, or hidden identity stitching

## Invariants

The suite-wide invariants are:

- provenance invariant:
  every consequential artifact must be traceable back to bounded source
  material or explicit promotion receipts
- expansion invariant:
  compressed or summarized outputs must expand back to raw identifiers,
  anchors, and supporting evidence
- context invariant:
  consequential outputs must retain or reference a usable context envelope
- authority invariant:
  only explicit promotion creates truth-bearing canonical records
- replay invariant:
  repeated runs over the same bounded inputs should preserve stable results
  within the same profile/version
- derived-only invariant:
  graph, latent, ranking, and inspection layers remain derived unless
  explicitly promoted
- append-only state invariant:
  historical state is preserved rather than silently rewritten
- anti-panopticon invariant:
  the system does not transform memory/context breadth into hidden person-risk
  scoring, covert identity fusion, or manipulative inference

## Preconditions

For a bounded suite flow to be admissible:

- source artifacts exist and are addressable
- provenance and identity fields are present enough to anchor the flow
- context envelope is available where the output would otherwise become
  context-free
- the authority class of each write path is known
- any cross-system adapter declares whether it is capture, archive, review,
  state, derived analysis, or promoted truth

## Postconditions

For a bounded suite flow to be considered complete:

- emitted artifacts are replayable and inspectable
- canonical writes, if any, are backed by promotion receipts
- derived products are explicitly marked as derived
- unresolved pressure remains visible as abstain / hold / follow-needed, not
  flattened away
- downstream operators can inspect why the output exists and what supports it
- cross-project handoff surfaces preserve IDs, lineage, and versioned profile
  assumptions

## Smart-Journal Flow

The original smart-journal idea becomes the following suite flow:

1. capture:
   audio, text, logs, browser/app traces, notes, exports, public sources
2. archive:
   store them in a canonical local memory/archive substrate
3. canonicalize:
   normalize identities, timestamps, envelopes, anchors, and dedupe
4. compile:
   produce read-only daily/state/context surfaces
5. review:
   extract bounded structured observations and legal/normative facts where
   appropriate
6. promote:
   create truth-bearing records only through explicit gates
7. derive:
   build packets, graphs, comparisons, dashboards, and operator views
8. union:
   combine bounded promoted/derived products across domains and legal systems
9. inspect:
   let operators challenge, compare, follow, or abstain

This is the suite-level moonshot. The legal moonshot is one major proving
ground inside it, not the whole thing.
